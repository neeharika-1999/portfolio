Clinical text classification with BioBERT

Can a biomedical language model read clinical abstracts and correctly identify the disease category? Fine-tuned BioBERT on a dataset of 29,000+ medical abstracts across five conditions, exploring the interplay between synthetic data augmentation and transformer-based embeddings.

Key finding

Fine-tuning BioBERT did not consistently improve downstream classifier performance — and in two of three cases, slightly reduced accuracy. The culprit: synthetic data generated via GPT lacked the nuance of real clinical notes, introducing label noise that diluted the embeddings. Random Forest was the only model to benefit from fine-tuning, gaining 13 points, suggesting ensemble methods are more resilient to noisy training signals.
